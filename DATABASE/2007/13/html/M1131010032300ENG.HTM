Inspection Procedure 4: Starting impossible (Starter not operative)


M11310100323ENG0000010000




COMMENT ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM



  • Failure is possibly caused by failed starter itself or failed related circuit.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Failed battery
  • Failed inhibitor switch
  • Failed starter motor
  • Open/short circuit in starter associated circuit or loose connector contact

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. Check battery voltage.



  • Measure battery voltage at cranking.
OK: 8 V or higher

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2 .
Check battery (Refer to GROUP 54A - Battery - On-vehicle Service - Battery Test ).

STEP 2. M.U.T.-III data list



  • Refer to Data List Reference Table .

    1. Item 02: Cranking signal
OK:
    ON (Ignition switch: ST)
    OFF (Ignition switch: ON)

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 11 .
Go to Step 3 .

STEP 3. Connector check: D-214 engine-A/T-ECU connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 4 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 4. Perform voltage measurement at D-214 engine-A/T-ECU connector.




  • Disconnect connector, and measure at the harness side.
  • Ignition switch: ST
  • Voltage between terminal No. 51 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 10 .
Go to Step 5 .

STEP 5. Connector check: C-05 inhibitor switch connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 6 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 6. Perform voltage measurement at C-05 inhibitor switch connector.




  • Disconnect connector, and measure at the harness side.
  • Ignition switch: ST
  • Voltage between terminal No. 10 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 9 .
Go to Step 7 .

STEP 7. Connector check: D-309 ignition switch connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 8 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 8. Check ignition switch.





  • Check ignition switch (Refer to GROUP 54A - Ignition switch - Inspection ).

Q. Is the check result normal?

Check intermediate connector D-226 and repair if necessary. If intermediate connector is normal, check and repair harness between C-05 (terminal No. 10) inhibitor switch connector and D-309 (terminal No. 5) ignition switch connector.

  • Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
Replace the ignition switch.

STEP 9. Checking inhibitor switch.





  • Check ignition switch (Refer to GROUP 23A - On-vehicle Service - A/T Control Component Check - Inhibitor Switch Check ).

Q. Is the check result normal?

Check intermediate connectors C-03 and D-224, and repair if necessary. If intermediate connector are normal, check and repair harness between C-05 (terminal No. 9) inhibitor switch connector and D-214 (terminal No. 51) engine-A/T-ECU connector.

  • Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
Replace the ignition switch.

STEP 10. Connector check: C-05 inhibitor switch starter connector and B-117 starter connector




Q. Is the check result normal?

Check intermediate connectors C-03, and repair if necessary. If intermediate connector is normal, check and repair harness between C-05 (terminal No. 9) inhibitor switch connector and B-117 (terminal No. 1) starter connector.

  • Check output line for short circuit.
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 11. Connector check: B-117 starter connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 12 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 12. Perform voltage measurement at B-117 starter connector.




  • Disconnect connector, and measure at the harness side.
  • Ignition switch: ST
  • Voltage between terminal No. 1 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 14 .
Go to Step 13 .

STEP 13. Connector check: C-05 inhibitor switch connector




Q. Is the check result normal?

Check intermediate connectors C-03, and repair if necessary. If intermediate connector is normal, check and repair harness between C-05 (terminal No. 9) inhibitor switch connector and B-117 (terminal No. 1) starter connector.

  • Check output line for open circuit and damage.
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 14. Connector check: B-118 starter connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 15 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 15. Perform voltage measurement at B-118 starter connector.




  • Disconnect connector, and measure at the harness side.
  • Voltage between terminal No. 1 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Does trouble symptom persist?

Go to Step 16 .
Check and repair harness between B-118 (terminal No. 1) starter connector and battery.

  • Check power supply line for open/short circuit.

STEP 16. Check harness between C-05 (terminal No. 9) inhibitor switch connector and B-117 (terminal No. 1) starter connector.




noteBefore checking harness, check intermediate connector C-03 and repair if necessary.

  • Check output line for damage.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 17 .
Repair the damaged harness wire.

STEP 17. Check harness between B-118 (terminal No. 1) starter connector and battery.




  • Check power supply line for damage.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Replace starter.
Repair the damaged harness wire.
