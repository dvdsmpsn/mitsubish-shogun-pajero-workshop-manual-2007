FUEL PRESSURE CONTROL


M21303000292ENG0000010000


Engine-ECU maintains a high fuel pressure by controlling the rail pressure in accordance with the signals input from various sensors. Accordingly, the injection of fuel at a high pressure improves combustion and reduces the amount of discharge of the particulate matter.

System Configuration Diagram

Engine-ECU calculates the target rail pressure in accordance with the engine speed and the fuel injection amount. To this value, the following compensations are made to calculate the target rail pressure that is suitable for the driving conditions. Then, the engine-ECU compares the target rail pressure with the actual rail pressure, and actuates the suction control valve so that the actual rail pressure will attain the target rail pressure (feedback control).

Engine coolant temperature compensation
This compensation is made to suit the engine coolant temperature. The lower the engine coolant temperature, the higher the rail pressure will be.
