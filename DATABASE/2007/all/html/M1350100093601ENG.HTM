INSPECTION PROCEDURE 3: Diagnosis code No.53 (pump motor clogging), or No.55 or No.57 is output, but no sound comes from HBB buzzer.


M13501000936ENG0000010000




OPERATION



  • The ASTC-ECU controls the buzzer by turning the power transistor in the ASTC-ECU ON and OFF.
  • The ASTC-ECU outputs the buzzer-sounding signal at the malfunction of the hydraulic brake booster (HBB). (Also the ASTC-ECU turns on the brake warning light at the same time.)
  • The buzzer will sound under the following cases:

    1. When the pump motor is locked (DTC 53 is set).
    2. When the pump motor is energized abnormally for long period (Diagnosis code No.55 is set).
    3. When the accumulator pressure is abnormally low (Diagnosis code No.57 is set).
    4. When the solenoid valve temperature in the ASTC-ECU is abnormally high (Diagnosis code is not set).

COMMENT ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM


The cause is probably an open circuit at the buzzer circuit, a malfunction of the buzzer or a failure of buzzer drive transistor in the ASTC-ECU.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Damaged wiring harness or connector

  • Malfunction of the buzzer

  • Malfunction of the ASTC-ECU

DIAGNOSIS


STEP 1. Check buzzer connector D-102 and ASTC-ECU connector D-232 for loose, corroded or damaged terminals, or terminals pushed back in the connector.






Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2.
Repair the faulty connector or terminal and then go to Step 8.

STEP 2. Check the buzzer circuit at ASTC-ECU harness connector D-232.




(1)Disconnect ASTC-ECU connector D-232 and check at the harness connector (component side).
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3)Connect terminal 54 to body ground.

Q. Does the buzzer sound?

Replace the ASTC-ECU (Refer to GROUP 35C, ASTC-ECU ). Then go to Step 8.
Go to Step 3.

(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
