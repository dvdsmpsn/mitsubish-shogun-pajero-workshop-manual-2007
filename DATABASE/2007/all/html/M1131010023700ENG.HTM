Code No. P0500: Vehicle Speed Sensor System


M11310100237ENG0000010000




OPERATION



  • A power voltage of 5 V is applied to the vehicle speed sensor (terminal No. 3) from the engine-A/T-ECU (terminal No. 79).

FUNCTION



  • The vehicle speed sensor converts the vehicle speed to the voltage, and then input it into the engine-A/T-ECU

TROUBLE JUDGMENT


Check Conditions
  • 2 seconds later after the engine has started up.
  • Engine speed: 2,500 r/min or more.
  • Under the high load operation.
Judgment Criterion
  • The sensor output voltage remains unchanged (no pulse signal is inputted) for 4 seconds.

PROBABLE CAUSE



  • Failed vehicle speed sensor
  • Open/short circuit in vehicle speed sensor circuit or loose connector contact
  • Failed engine-A/T-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. Check the speed meter


Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2 .
Check the speed meter (Refer to GROUP 54A -Combination Meter Assembly - On-vehicle Service - Vehicle Speedmeter Check ).

STEP 2. M.U.T.-III data list



  • Refer to Data list reference table .

    1. Item 04: Vehicle speed sensor

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 3 .
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).

STEP 3. Check connector: D-216 engine-A/T-ECU connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 4 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 4. Check harness between D-216 (terminal No. 79) engine-A/T-ECU connector and C-16 (terminal No. 3) Vehicle speed sensor connector.




noteBefore checking harness, check intermediate connectors D-228 and D-230, and repair if necessary.

  • Check output line for open circuit and damage.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 5 .
Repair the damaged harness wire.

STEP 5. Check the trouble symptoms.


Q. Does trouble symptom persist?

Replace the engine-A/T-ECU.
