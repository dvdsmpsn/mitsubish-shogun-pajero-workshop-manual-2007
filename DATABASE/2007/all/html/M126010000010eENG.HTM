GENERAL INFORMATION

The front axle consists of front hubs, knuckles, wheel bearings, driveshafts, and inner shaft, and it has the following features:

  • A free-wheel mechanism has been adopted which allows selection between 2WD and 4WD to be made while driving.
  • The wheel bearings used are double tapered roller type unit bearings with strong transverse load bearing ability.
  • The EUJ-EDJ constant velocity joint is adopted for the drive shaft to reduce the weight.
  • The resin boot is adopted for the drive shaft boot to reduce the thermal and aging deterioration.
  • Due to the use of the inner shaft, the right and left driveshafts are approximately the same in length. This reduces noise, vibration and torque steer.
  • The pack seal at the wheel bearing has a seal lip to avoid dirty water ingress <Short wheelbase>.
  • A separate oil seal is fitted at the wheel bearing to avoid dirty water ingress <Long wheelbase>.
  • The dust cover is tightened together with the knuckle to reduce water ingress into the wheel <Long wheelbase>.
  • ABS rotors for detecting the wheel speed are press-fitted to the EUJ outer race.
note
    EUJ: High Efficiency Compact Undercut Joint: the lighter and smaller constant velocity joint compared with the conventional BJ has been achieved by adopting the eight small balls instead of six balls.
    EDJ: High Efficiency Compact Double Offset Joint: the lighter and smaller constant velocity joint compared with the conventional DOJ has been achieved by adopting the eight small balls instead of six balls.
