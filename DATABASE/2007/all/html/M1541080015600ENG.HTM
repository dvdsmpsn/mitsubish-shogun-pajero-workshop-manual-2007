Inspection Procedure 4: The Navigation System can be Operated while the Vehicle is Driven.


M15410800156ENG0000010000


COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM


There is a failure in the wiring harness between the combination meter and the audio visual navigation unit, the respective connector(s), the combination meter or the audio visual navigation unit.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Malfunctions of combination meter
  • Malfunctions of audio visual navigation unit
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. Perform "Vehicle Signal Check" in the MMCS service mode.


Perform "Vehicle Signal Check" in the MMCS service mode to check whether vehicle speed signal is normal. (Refer to .)

Q. Is the vehicle speed signal transmitted normally?

Replace the audio visual navigation unit.
Go to Step 2.

STEP 2. Check the speedometer.


Check whether the speedometer works normally.

Q. Does the speedometer work normally?

Go to Step 3.
Diagnose the combination meter. Refer to Group 54Ab - Troubleshooting .

STEP 3. Connector check: C-16 vehicle speed sensor connector, D-210 audio visual navigation unit connector





Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 4.
Repair the defective connector.

STEP 4. Check the wire harness between C-16 vehicle speed sensor connector terminal No.3 and D-210 audio visual navigation unit connector terminal No.13.






  • Check the communication lines for open circuit.


notePrior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connector D-228, and repair if necessary.

Q. Is the wiring harness between C-16 vehicle speed sensor connector terminal No.3 and D-210 audio visual navigation unit connector terminal No.13 in good condition?

Go to Step 5.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 5. Substitute a known good audio visual navigation unit, and check the trouble symptom.


Check that no menus are active during driving.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Replace the audio visual navigation unit.
Replace the combination meter.
