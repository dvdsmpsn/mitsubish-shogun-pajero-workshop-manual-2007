REMOVAL SERVICE POINTS


<<A>> AXLE NUT REMOVAL



cautionDo not apply pressure to the wheel bearing by the vehicle weight to avoid possible damage when the axle nut is loosened.
Use special tool front hub & flange yoke holder (MB990767) to fix hub and remove the axle nut.

<<B>> CALIPER ASSEMBLY REMOVAL


Secure the removed caliper assembly with wire, etc.

<<C>> BRAKE DISC REMOVAL



If the brake disc is seized, install a M8 × 1.25 mm bolts as shown, and remove the disc by tightening the bolts evenly and gradually.

<<D>> TIE ROD END/UPPER ARM/LOWER ARM DISCONNECTION



1.
caution
  • Do not remove the nut from ball joint. Loosen it and use special tool to avoid possible damage to ball joint threads.
  • Hang special tool with cord to prevent it from falling.
Install special tool ball joint remover (MB991897 or MB992011) as shown in the figure.

2. Turn the bolt and knob as necessary to make the jaws of special tool parallel, tighten the bolt by hand and confirm that the jaws are still parallel.
noteWhen adjusting the jaws in parallel, make sure the knob is in the position shown in the figure.
3. Tighten the bolt with a wrench to disconnect the tie rod end.

INSTALLATION SERVICE POINTS


>>A<< UPPER ARM, HUB AND KNUCKLE ASSEMBLY CONNECTION


If the upper arm ball joint is disengaged from the knuckle, always renew the upper arm ball joint dust cover and retainer. (Refer to GROUP 33-Upper Arm.)

>>B<< AXLE NUT INSTALLATION



1.
cautionDo not apply pressure to wheel bearing by the vehicle weight to avoid possible damage when axle nut is loosened.
Using special tool front hub and flange yoke holder (MB990767), tighten the driveshaft nut.

>>C<< AXLE CAP INSTALLATION



1. If axle cap is as shown after it is fitted, turn it at least one notch clockwise to align it with the split pin hole.

2. If the axle cap is as shown, turn it at least one notch anticlockwise to align it with the split pin hole.
3. If the split pin hole is not visible, turn it at least three notches clockwise to align it with the split pin hole.
