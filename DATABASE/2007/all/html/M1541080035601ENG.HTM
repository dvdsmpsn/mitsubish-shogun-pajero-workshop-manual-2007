Inspection Procedure 2: No Sound is Heard. <Vehicles with Audio Amplifier>


M15410800356ENG0000010001




COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM


If the audio sound is not output, the audio visual navigation unit, audio amplifier, or power supply circuit of audio amplifier may have a problem, or the option coding information may be inconsistent.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Malfunctions of audio visual navigation unit
  • Malfunction of audio amplifier
  • Option coding information inconsistency
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. Connector check: E-37 audio amplifier connector



Q. Is audio amplifier connector E-37 in good condition?

Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace the damaged component(s).

STEP 3. Resistance measurement at audio amplifier connector E-37.



(1)Disconnect audio amplifier connector E-37, and measure the resistance available at the wiring harness side of the connector.

(2)Measure the resistance between terminal 24 and ground.
OK: Continuity exists (2 ohms or less)

(3)Measure the resistance between terminal 31 and ground.
OK: Continuity exists (2 ohms or less)

(4)Measure the resistance between terminal 32 and ground.
OK: Continuity exists (2 ohms or less)

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 5.
Go to Step 4.

STEP 4. Check the wiring harness between audio amplifier connector E-37 (terminal 24, 31, 32) and ground.




  • Check the ground wires for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?

The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 5. Voltage measurement at E-37 audio amplifier connector.



(1)Disconnect audio amplifier connector E-37, and measure the voltage available at the wiring harness-side connector.

(2)Measure the voltage between terminal 25 and ground.
OK: System voltage

(3)Measure the voltage between terminal 35 and ground.
OK: System voltage

(4)Measure the voltage between terminal 36 and ground.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 7.
Go to Step 6.

STEP 6. Check the wiring harness between audio amplifier connector E-37 (terminal 25, 35, 36) and fusible link (29).





notePrior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connectors D-236 and D-238, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the power supply line for open circuit and short circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 7.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 7. Check audio visual navigation unit connector D-204 and audio amplifier connector E-38 for loose, corroded or damaged terminals, or terminals pushed back in the connector.





Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 8.
Repair the defective connector.

STEP 8. Check the wiring harness between audio visual navigation unit connector D-204 (terminal 102) and audio amplifier connector E-38 (terminal 3)






notePrior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connector D-237, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the communication lines for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?


    Go to Step 9.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 9. Retest the system


Replace the audio amplifier, then check that the audio sound is output.

Q. Is the check result normal?

The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the audio visual navigation unit.
