Code No. P2100: Throttle Valve Control Servo Circuit (open)


M11310100260ENG0000010000





OPERATION



  • Controls the current that is applied from the engine-A/T-ECU (terminals No. 133, No. 141) to the electronic-controlled throttle valve (terminals No. 5, No. 6).

FUNCTION



  • Engine-A/T-ECU varies the direction and the amperage of the current that is applied to the throttle valve control servo in order to control the opening of the throttle valve.

TROUBLE JUDGMENT


Check Condition
  • Battery positive voltage is 8.3 V or higher.
Judgement Criterion
  • Throttle actuator control motor current is 0.1 A or less for 0.72 second.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Failed throttle valve control servo.
  • Open/short circuit in throttle valve control servo circuit or lose connector contact.
  • Failed engine-A/T-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. Connector check: B-31 electronic-controlled throttle valve connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 2. Perform resistance measurement at B-31 electronic-controlled throttle valve connector.




  • Disconnect connector, and measure at electronic-controlled throttle valve side.
  • Resistance between terminal No. 5 and No. 6.
OK: 0.3 - 100 kΩ (at 20 °C)

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 3 .
Replace the throttle body assembly.

STEP 3. Connector check: D-218 engine-A/T-ECU connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 4 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 4. Perform resistance measurement at D-218 engine-A/T-ECU connector.




  • Disconnect connector and measure at harness side.
  • Resistance between terminal No. 144, No. 145 and earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 7 .
Go to Step 5 .

STEP 5. Connector check: D-14 earth connector



Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 6 .
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 6. Check harness between D-218 engine-A/T-ECU and D-14 earth connector.





  1. Check harness between D-218 (terminal No. 144) engine-A/T-ECU and D-14 (terminal No. 6) earth connector.
  2. Check harness between D-218 (terminal No. 145) engine-A/T-ECU and D-14 (terminal No. 6) earth connector.

Q. Are the check results normal?

Go to Step 7 .
Repair

STEP 7. Check harness between B-31 (terminal No. 6) electronic-controlled throttle valve connector and D-218 (terminal No. 133) engine-A/T-ECU connector.





  • Check output line for short circuit and damage.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 8 .
Repair the damaged harness wire.

STEP 8. Check harness between B-31 (terminal No. 5) electronic-controlled throttle valve connector and D-218 (terminal No. 141) engine-A/T-ECU connector.





  • Check output line for short circuit and damage.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 9 .
Repair the damaged harness wire.

STEP 9. Check the trouble symptoms.


Q. Is the check result normal?

Replace the engine-A/T-ECU.
