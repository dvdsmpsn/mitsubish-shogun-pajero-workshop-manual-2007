REAR PROPELLER SHAFT


The carbon fiber reinforced plastic resin tube has been adopted for the rear propeller shaft of all models.The strength of carbon reinforced plastic resin tube is set so as to break along the longitudinal axis when the load exceeding the limit is applied.
When the impact load is imposed to the propeller shaft via the engine and transmission at front collision, the carbon reinforced plastic resin tube is broken along the longitudinal axis to allow the engine and transmission move rearward and thus sufficient crushable length is secured.


POWER WINDOW AND SUNROOF WITH SAFETY MECHANISM


If a trapped hand, hand, or other item is detected while the sunroof or any window is closing, the motor direction is automatically reversed for safety.

IMPACT SAFETY BODY RISE(REINFORCED IMPACT SAFETY EVOLUTION)


The front and rear structures to absorb high energy, and the highly tough cabin structure is adopted to reduce the risk of passenger injuries at front-, rear-, and side-impact collisions, secure the space for life protection, and facilitate rescuing passengers. The structures also have the following features:



  1. The crush box structure has been adopted to the front end of the sidemember to improve the characteristics at a minor frontal collision.
  2. Closed-crosssectional construction has been used for the front roof rail, the roof bow center and the rear roof rail to improve vehicle body ridigity and suppress vibration and noise.
  3. An annular construction has been used for the side structure reinforcement to improve collision safety and vehicle body ridigity.
  4. The front frame structure is supported in three directions by the dash crossmember center, dash crossmember lower, and front sidemember rear in order to improve the frontal collision characteristics, and increase the vehicle body rigidity.
  5. The front sidemember structure has been changed to a straight frame structure with a hexagon cross section in order to improve the collision characteristics.
  6. The padding structure of the fender bracket has been adopted to efficiently absorb energy upon impact and improve the pedestrian protection capability.
  7. The impact absorbing opening on the cowl top outer panel has been added to efficiently absorb energy upon impact and improve the pedestrian protection capability.

SUPPLEMENTAL RESTRAINT SYSTEM (SRS)


The driver’s and passenger’s (front) air bags, side-airboats, curtain air bags and seat belts with pre-tensioner have been standard equipped on all vehicles.
Dual stage air bag system has been adopted to both driver and front passenger sides. This is consisted of the front impact sensor, SRS-ECU and dual stage inflator. Air bag is deployed in two stages according to the strength of impact received.
An inflator that does not contain sodium azide has been adopted for all types of the air bag modules.


SRS FRONT AIR BAG


Front air bag is designed to supplement the front seat belts. It eliminates or reduces injury to the front passenger(s) by deploying air bag(s) in case of a frontal collision.

SRS SIDE-AIRBAG


Side-airbag systems in the front seats are activated when sideward impacts applied to the vehicle exceed a threshold to protect the occupants’ upper bodies.

SRS CURTAIN AIR BAG


The curtain air bag systems are activated when sideward impacts applied to the vehicle exceed a threshold, to protect the heads of the occupants in the front and second seats.

SEAT BELT WITH PRE-TENSIONER


The seat belts with pre-tensioner work simultaneously with the SRS. The seat belt incorporating the pre-tensioner automatically winds the seatbelt upon front impact to reduce forward shifting of the driver’s and passenger’s.
