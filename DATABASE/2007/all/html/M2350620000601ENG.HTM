ACTIVE STABILITY CONTROL SYSTEM ON (EXAMPLE: TRUNING TO LEFT, OVERSTEER SUPPRESSED)




If ASTC-ECU determines that the vehicle tends to be oversteer, the signals indicated in the table shown below are output to each solenoid valve of ABS and active stability control system in order to regulate pressurization of outward front wheels.
To be specific, oil pressurized by pump makes the switch solenoid valve (STR) and the switch solenoid valve (SA1) open and goes through them to pressurize the front right wheel. At the moment, the control solenoid valve at the front right wheel (IN) is closed to block supply of fluid pressure.

ACTIVE STABILITY CONTROL SYSTEM ON (EXAMPLE: TURNING TO LEFT, UNDERSTEER SUPPRESSED)




If ASTC-ECU determines that the vehicle tends to be understeer, the signals indicated in the table shown below are output to each solenoid valve of ABS and active stability control system in order to regulate pressurization of rear inside wheels.
To be specific, oil pressurized by pump goes through the switch solenoid valve (STR) and the switch solenoid valve (SA3) to pressurize the cylinder at the rear left wheel. At the moment, the control solenoid valve at the rear right wheel (IN) is closed to block supply of fluid pressure.

ACTIVE TRACTION CONTROL SYSTEM ON


If freewheel is determined by the ASTC-ECU, pressurization is controlled over freewheeling wheels so that torque can be transmitted to wheels that are not subject to freewheel. Drive of each solenoid valve is in the same way as active stability control system operation.
