WHEEL SPEED SENSOR



The wheel speed sensors consist of fixed ABS speed sensors and the ABS rotors that rotate at the same speed as the wheels, and output alternating voltage signals at frequencies which are proportional to the wheel speed.

  • The ABS rotors (50 teeth) are installed to the drive shafts at both front and rear, and the ABS speed sensors are installed to knuckles.
  • The gap between the ABS rotors and the ABS speed sensors are non-adjustable at both the front and rear to improve serviceability.

G&YAW RATE SENSOR



This sensor is mounted under the center console, and detects lateral and longitudinal acceleration and yaw-rate of the vehicle.

STEERING WHEEL SENSOR



This sensor is mounted behind the column switch, and detects the steering wheel angle.

ASC OFF SWITCH




This switch is mounted on the lower panel, and turns off and on the system.

2WD DETECTION SWITCH, 2WD/4WD DETECTION SWITCH, 4H DETECTION SWITCH, CENTER DIFFERENTIAL LOCK DETECTION SWITCH, 4LLc DETECTION SWITCH


These switches are also used for the 4WD system, and output drive system state to the ASTC-ECU. The ASTC-ECU can change the control according to the drive system state with this signal.

REAR DIFFERENTIAL LOCK DETECTION SWITCH <VEHICLES WITH DIFFERENTIAL LOCK (OPTION)>



This switch is also used by rear differential lock system. Rear differential lock indicator signal is also sent to combination meter. The ASTC-ECU disables active stability control, active traction control and ABS (EBD) control when rear differential lock switch ON or rear differential lock indicator flash or illuminate to avoid engine power loss and certain braking force.

STOP LAMP SWITCH


This switch turns on when the brake pedal is depressed, and turns off when the brake is released. The ASTC-ECU detects whether the stoplamp switch is on or off by means of fluctuations in voltage (ON: system voltage; OFF: Approxmatery 0 V). This data is used for ASTC-ECU fail-safe/diagnosis function.
