REGULATOR VALVE


The regulator valve maintains the hydraulic pressure from the oil pump at a constant line pressure.

[Operation]
  1. Parking and Neutral
    Hydraulic pressure from the oil pump is supplied to the regulator valve via port A, port B. Hydraulic pressure supplied via port A is then fed to the torque converter pressure control valve (and thence to the torque converter) via port C. Also, line pressure that has passed through the manual valve is supplied to the regulator valve via port D. When line pressure is supplied via port B, port D, and port E, the surface area of land #3 causes the valve to be pushed toward the left. The counteraction of this force by the spring effectively regulates the line pressure. Any increase in engine speed (and thus also in oil pump speed) causes a concomitant increase in the hydraulic pressure acting upon port B, port D, and port E. This increased pressure overcomes the spring and pushes the valve toward the left. The fluid passage leading to the torque converter (labelled "a" in the drawing) is thus widened, and more fluid is supplied to the torque converter.
    Any further increase in hydraulic pressure causes the valve to be pushed further toward the left. When this occurs, fluid passage "b" is opened. This allows pressure to escape to the oil pump side and thus lowers the line pressure. Any decrease in the line pressure causes a concomitant decrease in the hydraulic pressure acting upon port B, port D, and port E. When this occurs, the spring pushes the valve back toward the right, thereby closing fluid passage "b." The overall effect of this mechanism is to maintain the line pressure at a constant level. If the line pressure exceeds the regulated pressure from the regulator valve, the relief valve in line A allows pressure to escape and thereby protects the hydraulic circuit.
  2. First and Second Gears
    Line pressure is supplied via port A and port B. Thus, the leftward force on the valve results from the line pressure multiplied by the difference between the surface areas of land #1 and land #3. The effective surface area of only land #4 is smaller than in 1 [parking and neutral], so there is less leftward force on the valve per unit of pressure. As a result, fluid passage "b" stays open under greater pressure than in 1, causing a concomitant increase in line pressure. The regulator valve responds to increases in line pressure as described in 1.
  3. Third, Fourth and Fifth Gears
    Line pressure is supplied via port F in addition to that supplied from port B and port D. Thus the leftward force on the valve is that caused by the surface area difference between land #1 and land #4. Land #4 has a greater surface area than land #3, so the leftward force on the valve per unit of pressure is greater than in 2 [1st and 2nd gears]. As a result fluid passage "b" opens under less pressure than in 2, causing a concomitant reduction in line pressure. The regulator valve responds to increases in line pressure as described in 1.
  4. Reverse
    When the "R" range is selected, thus cutting the supply of line pressure to port D. Therefore, the leftward force on the valve is that resulting from the surface area difference between land #1 and land #2. Since land #1 has a smaller surface area than land #2, the leftward force acting on the valve per unit of pressure is smaller than in 2 [1st and 2nd gears]. As a result, fluid passage "b" does not open unless the pressure is higher than in 2, and the line pressure increases concomitantly. Thus, the high line pressure required for reversing is applied to the operating elements. The regulator valve responds to increases in line pressure as described in 1.

Torque Converter Pressure Control Valve


The torque converter pressure control valve keeps constant the fluid pressure to the torque converter (when the damper clutch is disengaged) and the pressure of the lubricating oil.


[Operation]
  1. Surplus fluid after adjustment of the line pressure by the regulator valve is supplied to the torque converter by the torque converter pressure control valve. At this time, some of the hydraulic pressure from line A passes through an orifice and is supplied via port B to a chamber on the right-hand side of the valve. The hydraulic pressure in this chamber counteracts the force of the spring, thereby moving the valve and regulating the torque converter pressure. When the chamber pressure is weaker than the spring force, the valve is pushed toward the right and hydraulic pressure from the regulator valve is supplied to the torque converter.
  2. Any increase in hydraulic pressure from the regulator valve causes a concomitant increase in chamber pressure. When the chamber pressure overcomes the spring force, the valve is pushed toward the left. This causes port C to open, which enables pressure to escape to the oil pump side and thereby reduces the hydraulic pressure. Any decrease in hydraulic pressure causes a concomitant decrease in chamber pressure. When the spring force overcomes the chamber pressure, it pushes the valve back toward the right, thereby closing port C. Thus, the torque converter pressure is constantly regulated and prevented from exceeding a fixed level.

Damper Clutch Control Valve and Damper Clutch Solenoid Valve



  • The damper clutch solenoid valve controls the hydraulic pressure acting upon the damper clutch control valve and damper clutch.
  • The damper clutch solenoid valve is duty-controlled by signals from the engine-A/T-ECU, converting these electric signals into hydraulic pressure.


[Operation]
  1. When the damper clutch solenoid valve is OFF and the damper clutch is disengaged, line pressure acts upon port A and port B. Thus, the sum of the force acting upon land #7 and the force of the spring overcomes the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting on the surface area difference between land #2 and land #3. As a result, the valve is pushed toward the right. This causes hydraulic pressure from the torque converter pressure control valve to enter line C from line D and be supplied to the space between the torque converter's front cover and the damper clutch. Thus, the damper clutch does not operate, and the torque converter operates normally.
  2. Within the damper clutch operating range, the damper clutch solenoid valve is duty-controlled by commands from the A/T-ECU, and the hydraulic pressure supplied to port to port A is decreased. Thus, the sum of the force acting upon land #7 and the force of the spring is overcome by the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting on the surface area difference between land #2 and land #3. As a result, the valve is pushed toward the left. This allows pressure from the torque converter pressure control valve to escape from line D to the oil cooler via line E. At the same time, line pressure in line F enters the torque converter from line G via line H. Thus, hydraulic pressure acts between the damper clutch and turbine, the damper clutch is pressed against the front cover, and the damper clutch operates.

Manual valve



  • The manual valve is interlocked with the selector lever next to the drivers seat. In accordance with the selector lever position, the manual valve switches fluid from one passage to another and thus feeds line pressure to the relevant valves.
  • The selector lever has four positions of P, R, N and D, but the manual valve has only three positions of R, P-N and D because the valve positions are the same for P and N due to the action of the detente lever.



[Operation]
  1. With the manual valve in the N·P position port A and port B are open such that line pressure is supplied to the regulator valve and fail-safe valve A.
  2. With the manual valve in the D position, port C and port D are open, in addition to the valves in step 1 [N·P position], oil pressure is supplied to each solenoid valve and pressure control valve of the DCC clutch, the 2ND brake, the UD clutch, the OD clutch and the fail safe valve C.
  3. With the manual valve in the R position, port E is open. Line pressure is thus supplied to the reverse clutch and fail-safe valve B.

PRESSURE CONTROL VALVES AND SOLENOID VALVES



  • With the exception of the reverse clutch, there is one pressure control valve and one solenoid valve for every clutch and brake. The direct clutch uses either of the pressure control valve and the solenoid valve for the low-reverse brake by switching the switch valve.
  • Under the control of the solenoid valves, the pressure control valves regulate the hydraulic pressure acting upon the elements and thus prevent the occurrence of shock during gear shifts.
  • The solenoid valves are duty-controlled by signals from the engine-A/T-ECU, converting these electric signals into hydraulic pressure.

[OPERATION]


The pressure control valves vary in shape but all operate in the same manner. By way of example, the following section describes the operation of the OD clutch arrangement.



  1. When the solenoid valve is ON and the OD clutch is not operational, the fluid passage from the solenoid valve is closed, so there is no hydraulic pressure in line A. At this time, the pressure control valve is pushed toward the right by the force of its spring. Thus, port B is closed, and no hydraulic pressure is supplied to the OD clutch.
  2. When the OD clutch becomes operational, the A/T-ECU instructs the solenoid valve to depress the check ball. This opens the fluid passage to line A, thus supplying hydraulic pressure to the pressure control valve. When hydraulic pressure is supplied from line A to the pressure control valve, the leftward force on the valve resulting from the surface area difference between land #1 and land #2 overcomes the force of the spring and pushes the valve toward the left. As a result, port B opens allowing the line pressure in line C to be supplied to the OD clutch. When the gear shift is complete, the solenoid valve is turned OFF, so the hydraulic pressure supplied to the OD clutch becomes equal to the line pressure.

SWITCH VAIVE



  • When the OD clutch operates, hydraulic pressure from the switch valve is supplied to the regulator valve. As a result, the line pressure is reduced in 3rd 4th and 5th gears.
  • When the control relay is OFF and the fail-safe mechanism operates, the supply of hydraulic pressure from the LR pressure control valve to the LR brake is cut.




[Operation]
  1. In gears other than 3rd, 4th and 5th, line pressure is supplied from port A to a chamber on the right-hand side of the valve. The valve is thus, pushed toward the left. Even when hydraulic pressure is supplied from the LR pressure control valve to port B, the valve does not move since land #2 and land #3 have the same surface area.
  2. In 3rd, 4th and 5th gears, hydraulic pressure is supplied also from port C to a chamber on the left hand side of the valve. Due to the surface area difference between land #1 and land #4, the valve is pushed toward the right. As a result, port D opens, and hydraulic pressure is supplied to the regulator valve. Since the port E is opened as well, when the oil pressure is supplied to port B, it is supplied to the direct clutch via the fail-safe valve C.
  3. When the fail-safe mechanism operates, the solenoid valve is turned OFF. Hydraulic pressure is thus supplied to port B. In 3rd gear, however, the valve is pushed toward the right irrespective of the LR solenoid valves condition. Also, land #2 and land #3 have the same surface area, so the valve does not move even if hydraulic pressure is supplied to port B.

FAIL-SAFE VALVE A



  • Fail-safe valve A releases the LR brake hydraulic pressure in the event of a fail-safe condition.
  • In reverse, fail-safe valve A changes the fluid passage to the LR brake, and thus achieves quick, smooth gear shifts.





[Operation]
  1. NEUTRAL AND FIRST GEAR
    Line pressure from the manual valve is supplied by port A to a chamber on the, right-hand side of the valve. Also, hydraulic pressure from the LR pressure control valve is supplied to port B via the switch valve. Thus, the force acting on land #1 overcomes the sum of the spring force and the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #2 and land #3. Valve A1 and valve A2 are thus pushed toward the left. As a result, port C opens, and hydraulic pressure from the LR pressure control valve is supplied to the LR brake.
  2. SECOND GEAR
    In addition to port A hydraulic pressure is supplied to port D. Thus, the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #1 overcomes the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #4 and land #5. As a result, valve A1 and valve A2 are pushed toward the left.
  3. THIRD GEAR AND FOURTH GEAR
    In addition to port A, hydraulic pressure is supplied to port E. Thus, the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #1 overcomes the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #6. As a result, valve A1 and valve A2 are pushed toward the left.
  4. FAIL-SAFE CONDITION AND FIFTH GEAR
    In the event of a fail-safe condition resulting from a fault in the 2ND or OD solenoid valve, hydraulic pressure is supplied to either or both of ports E and D in addition to the pressure supplied to port A and port B. Thus, the sum of <the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting on land #6 or from the surface area difference between land #4 and land #5> and <the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #2 and land #3> overcomes the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #1. As a result, valve A1 and valve A2 are pushed toward the right. The LR brake pressure is thus discharged, and an interlock is prevented. In 5th gear too, valve A1 and valve A2 are pushed toward the right in this manner.
  5. REVERSE
    In reverse, so no hydraulic pressure is supplied to port A. Hydraulic pressure is supplied only from port B. As a result, valve A2 is pushed toward the right by the spring force and the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #2 and land #3. Valve A is pushed toward the left by the spring force. As a result, the LR brake pressure passes through port C and is rapidly released from port F, and the supply pressure is connected to the LR brake via port G and port H. By switching the hydraulic pressure supply line to the LR brake and temporarily dropping the supply pressure to the LR brake, this arrangement enables quick, smooth control of N → R and P → R shifts.

FAIL-SAFE VALVE B


In the event of a fail-safe condition (when the control relay is OFF), fail-safe valve B cuts the supply of hydraulic pressure from the 2ND pressure control valve to the 2ND brake.







[Operation]
  1. NEUTRAL
    Line pressure is supplied from port A to a chamber on the right-hand side of the valve, so the valve is pushed toward the left.
  2. FIRST GEAR
    In addition to port A hydraulic pressure is supplied to port B. Thus, the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #1 overcomes the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #5 and land #6. As a result, the valve is pushed toward the left.
  3. SECOND GEAR
    In addition to port A, hydraulic pressure is supplied to port B and, from the 2ND pressure control valve, to port C. Thus, the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #1 overcomes the sum of <the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #2 and land #3> and <the force resulting from the surface difference between land #5 and land #6>. As a result, the valve is pushed toward the left. This causes port D to open, so hydraulic pressure is supplied from the 2ND pressure control valve to the 2ND brake.
  4. THIRD GEAR AND FOURTH GEAR
    In addition to port A, hydraulic pressure is supplied to port B. Pressure is supplied also to port E. Thus, the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #1 overcomes the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #4 and land #6. As a result, the valve is pushed toward the left.
  5. FIFTH GEAR
    In addition to port A, hydraulic pressure is supplied to port C and port E. Thus, the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #1 overcomes the sum of <the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon the surface area difference between land #2 and land #3> and <the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon the surface area difference between land #4 and land #5>. As a result, the valve is pushed toward the left. This causes port D to open, so hydraulic pressure is supplied from the 2ND pressure control valve to the 2ND brake.
  6. REVERSE
    In addition to port A, line pressure from the manual valve is supplied from port F to a chamber at the right-hand side of the valve. Thus, the surface areas of land #1 and land #6 cause the valve to be pushed to right.
  7. FAIL-SAFE OPERATION (CONTROL RELAY OFF)
    In the event of a fail-safe condition, the solenoid valve is turned OFF, so hydraulic pressure is supplied to port C. Thus, the force resulting from the hydraulic pressure acting upon land #1 overcomes the sum of <the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #2 and land #3> and <the force resulting from the surface area difference between land #4 and land #6>. As a result, port D is connected to port G the 2ND brake hydraulic pressure is released. Also, the hydraulic pressure from the 2ND pressure control valve is cut by fail-safe valve B.

FAIL-SAFE VALVE C


At fail-safe condition (control relay OFF), this valve cuts the oil pressure supply from the switch valve to the direct clutch.




[Operation]
  1. NEUTRAL AND REVERSE
    Since the line pressure is supplied from the port A to the left chamber of the valve, the valve is pushed right.
  2. FIRST GEAR, SECOND GEAR AND THIRD GEAR
    The line pressure is supplied from the port A and the port B. The force generated by oil pressure acting on the area difference of the land #1 and the land #2 overcomes the force generated by oil pressure acting on the land #4, therefore the valve is pushed left.
  3. FOURTH GEAR AND FIFTH GEAR
    Oil pressure is supplied to the port C in addition to the port B, but oil pressure is not supplied to the port A. Due to the force generated by oil pressure acting on the area difference of the land #1 and the land #3, the valve is pushed left. Thus the port D is opened to supply the oil pressure from the port C of the switch valve to the direct clutch.
  4. FAIL-SAFE OPERATION (CONTROL RELAY OFF)
    Oil pressure is supplied from port B, port C and port A. Since the land #3 and the land #4 have the same area, the valve is pushed right by the oil pressure acting on the land #1. Accordingly, the oil pressure existing in the port D is exhausted from port E and the oil pressure supply from the port C of the switch valve to the direct clutch is cut.
