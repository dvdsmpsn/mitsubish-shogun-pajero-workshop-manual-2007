FEATURES


The brake system has been designed to give greater reliability and durability and to provide excellent braking performance.
BRAKING PERFORMANCE
  • Adoption of a hydraulic brake booster (HBB) which maintains a high brake-assisting force even in high deceleration ranges.
  • Adoption of the 16-inch ventilated disc brake for short wheelbase vehicles and the 17-inch ventilated disc brake for long wheelbase vehicles, achieving stable braking force and improving braking feeling.
STABILITY
  • Adoption of the Active Stability and Traction Control System (ASTC) which integrates the Active Stability Control System with the Active Traction Control System: The Active Stability Control System responds to driving conditions and reduces the engine output and applies braking force to four wheels independently to control the vehicle behaviour, avoiding the critical state; The Active Traction Control System prevents the driving force loss during slipping of the driving wheels by automatically applying the brakes when driving on partly low-mu road surfaces such as off-road or when the driving wheels slip, and allows the vehicle to be rocked out of a hollow or a slippery surface, just like the differential lock.
  • Adoption of an electronic brake-force distribution(EBD) which makes it possible to maintain the maximum amount of braking force even when the vehicle's load is unevenly distributed.
  • Adoption of independent front and rear wheel brake lines (H piping).
  • Ventilated discs have been adopted for front wheel and rear wheel in order to improve anti-fading performance.
SERVICEABILITY
  • A diagnosis function has been adopted for the HBB and ASTC system in order to make inspection easier.
  • For the front and rear disc brakes, an outer disc method separated hub and rotor has been adopted to make removal and installation easier.
  • The master cylinder reservoir tank cap has been coloured white to make identification easier.
  • Parking brake cable adjustment is now carried out from inside the passenger compartment.
  • Reduction in number of brake pipes
