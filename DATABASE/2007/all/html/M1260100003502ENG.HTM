REMOVAL SERVICE POINTS


<<A>> AXLE NUT REMOVAL


cautionDo not apply pressure to wheel bearing by the vehicle weight to avoid possible damage when axle nut is loosened.

Use special tool front hub and flange yoke holder (MB990767) to fix the hub and remove the axle nut.

<<B>> CALIPER ASSEMBLY REMOVAL


Secure the removed caliper assembly with wire to prevent it from falling off.

<<C>> TIE ROD END /LOWER ARM BALL JOINT DISCONNECTION


caution
  • Do not remove the nut from the ball joint. Loosen it and use the special tool to avoid possible damage to ball joint threads.
  • Hang the special tool with a cord to prevent from falling.

1. Install special tool ball joint remover (MB991897 or MB992011) as shown in the figure.

2. Turn the bolt and knob as necessary to make the jaws of special tool parallel, tighten the bolt by hand and confirm that the jaws are still parallel.
noteWhen adjusting the jaws in parallel, make sure the knob is in the position shown in the figure.
3. Tighten the bolt with a wrench to disconnect the tie rod end.

INSTALLATION SERVICE POINTS


>>A<< DRIVESHAFT ASSEMBLY INSTALLATION



For long wheelbase, apply multipurpose grease as shown in the illustration before install the driveshaft assembly.

>>B<< AXLE NUT INSTALLATION


cautionBefore securely tightening the axle nuts, make sure there is no load on the wheel bearings. Otherwise the wheel bearings will be damaged.

1. Using special tool front hub and flange yoke holder (MB990767), tighten the axle nut. At this time, tighten the axle nut to the specified torque in expectation of final tightening.

>>C<< AXLE CAP INSTALLATION



1. If axle cap is as shown after it is fitted, turn it at least one notch clockwise to align it with the split pin hole.

2. If the axle cap is as shown, turn it at least one notch anticlockwise to align it with the split pin hole.
3. If the split pin hole is not visible, turn it at least three notches clockwise to align it with the split pin hole.
