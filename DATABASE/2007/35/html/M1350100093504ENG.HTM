INSPECTION PROCEDURE 2: After starting the engine, the brake warning lamp remains ON.


M13501000935ENG0000010000


cautionFirst of all, check the parking brake switch circuit and the brake fluid level switch circuit.
noteThis diagnosis procedure is limited to cases where communication with the scan tool is possible (ASTC-ECU power supply is normal) and no Diagnosis code sets.


OPERATION


When the power transistor in the ASTC-ECU is off or the circuit between the ASTC-ECU and the combination meter is open, the power transistor in the combination meter will turn on to illuminate the brake warning light.

COMMENT ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM


The cause is probably the ASTC-ECU malfunction or open circuit at the ASTC-ECU’s brake warning light drive circuit.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Damaged wiring harness or connector

  • Malfunction of the ASTC-ECU

  • Malfunction of the combination meter

DIAGNOSIS


STEP 1. Check combination meter connector D-02 and ASTC-ECU connector D-223 for loose, corroded or damaged terminals, or terminals pushed back in the connector.






Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2.
Repair the faulty connector or terminal and then go to Step 5.

STEP 2. Check the brake warning light drive circuit at ASTC-ECU harness connector D-223.




(1)Disconnect ASTC-ECU connector D-223 and check at the harness connector (component side).
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3)Connect terminal 81 to a body ground.

Q. Does the brake warning light turn off?

Replace the ASTC-ECU (Refer to GROUP 35C, ASTC-ECU ). Then go to Step 5.
Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. Check intermediate connector D-230 for loose, corroded or damaged terminals, or terminals pushed back in the connector.




Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace it and then go to Step 5.

STEP 4. Check the harness wires between combination meter connector D-02 (terminal 45) and ASTC-ECU connector D-223 (terminal 81).






Q. Is the check result normal?

Replace the combination meter (Refer to GROUP 54Ab, Combination Meter Assembly ). Then go to Step 5.
Repair or replace it and then go to Step 5.

STEP 5. Retest the system.


Q. Does the brake warning light turn on for three seconds and then turn off on start-up?

The procedure is complete.
Start over at Step 1.
