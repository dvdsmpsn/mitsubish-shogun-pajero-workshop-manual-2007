Diagnosis Item 9: Diagnose when the M.U.T.-III cannot receive the data sent by immobilizer-ECU.


M15408300014ENG0000010000


cautionWhen servicing a CAN bus line, earth yourself by touching a metal object such as an unpainted water pipe. If you fail to do, a component connected to the CAN bus line may be broken.


FUNCTION


The diagnostic result demonstrates that "the M.U.T.-III cannot receive the sent data from the immobilizer-ECU" when the M.U.T.-III checks the periodically sent data from each ECU and cannot receive the immobilizer-ECU data only.

TROUBLE JUDGEMENT CONDITIONS


M.U.T.-III judges the trouble when the periodically sent data from immobilizer-ECU cannot be received and sent.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Power supply circuit malfunction of the immobilizer-ECU
  • Malfunction of the immobilizer-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. Connector check: D-03 joint connector (CAN1) and D-126 immobilizer-ECU connector




cautionThe strand end of the twist wire should be within 10 cm from the connector. For details refer to .
When checking the joint connector, ensure that its wiring harness side and its short pins are not damaged.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2.
Repair the defective connector. Replace the joint connector as necessary.

STEP 2. Resistance measurement at D-03 joint connector (CAN1) and D-126 immobilizer-ECU connector.




cautionA digital multimeter should be used. For details refer to .
cautionThe test wiring harness should be used. For details refer to .
(1)Disconnect joint connector (CAN1) and the immobilizer-ECU connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the LOCK (OFF) position.

cautionWhen measuring the resistance, disconnect the negative battery terminal. For details refer to .
Ensure that the negative battery terminal is disconnected.

(4)Continuity between D-03 joint connector (CAN1) terminal No.5 and D-126 immobilizer-ECU connector terminal No.3
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)

(5)Continuity between D-03 joint connector (CAN1) terminal No.16 and D-126 immobilizer-ECU connector terminal No.4
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)

Q. Is the check result normal?

<All the resistances measure 2 Ω or less> Power supply to the immobilizer-ECU may be suspected. Diagnose the immobilizer-ECU. Refer to .
<Either or all of the resistances measure more than 2 Ω> Repair the wiring harness between joint connector (CAN1) and the immobilizer-ECU connector.
